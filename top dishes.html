Chef's list

Composed by our most prestige and highly acclaimed chefs and food critics, this is an intricatedly composed list of 100 best Vietnamese dish in 2021. The lists consists of descriptions, pictures, locations,... of the dish, ensuring the utmost convenience for you should you decide to have a taste.

Pho

Phở or pho is a Vietnamese soup dish consisting of broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat, sometimes chicken.
Banh Mi

In Vietnamese cuisine, Bánh mì or banh mi (/ˈbɑːn miː/,[2][3][4][5] /ˈbæn/;[6][5] Vietnamese: [ɓǎjŋ̟ mî], "bread") is a short baguette with thin, crisp crust and soft, airy texture. It is often split lengthwise and filled with savory ingredients like a submarine sandwich and served as a meal, called bánh mì thịt. Plain banh mi is also eaten as a staple food.
Banh Cuon

Bánh cuốn is made from a thin, wide sheet of fermented[2] rice batter filled with a mixture of cooked seasoned ground pork, minced wood ear mushroom, and minced shallots. Sides for this dish usually consist of chả lụa (Vietnamese pork sausage), sliced cucumber, and bean sprouts, with the dipping sauce which is fish sauce called nước chấm (Nuoc Mam).
Banh Beo

A bánh bèo is a Vietnamese dish that is originated from Huế, a city in Central Vietnam. The English translation for this dish is water fern cakes. Bánh bèo is made from a combination of rice flour and tapioca flour. It is popular street food in Vietnam. The ingredients include rice cake, dried shrimps, crispy pork skin, scallion oil, and dipping sauce.[1][self-published source] It is usually eaten as a snack but is now considered a dish in restaurants and can be eaten as lunch and dinner.
Banh Chung

Bánh chưng is a traditional Vietnamese food which is made from glutinous rice, mung beans, pork and other ingredients.
